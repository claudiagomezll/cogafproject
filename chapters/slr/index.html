Chapter One

A systematic literature review on serious games methodologies for training in the mining sector

This chapter aims to conduct a systematic literature review (SLR) on SG methodologies for the mining sector.

Systematic Literature Review

This systematic review was conducted using a methodology inspired by the PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) methodology. In this SLR were identified three research questions to learn how SGs facilitate immediate feedback, brain stimulation, and at-risk training. The outcome was the selection of 30 publications following a three-phase process: 1) A search string based on three research questions was defined and applied to some databases. 2) Publications were filtered by title and abstract. 3) A full-text reading was conducted for selecting relevant publications. The results of this SLR showed SG development methodologies with guided development processes that can be applied to any case study. In addition, it was found that Virtual Reality (VR), despite its implementation costs, is the most used technology for safety training, inspection, and operation of heavy machinery. Conclusions obtained from this SLR are the following:1) Modeling languages (e.g., GML, UML) and metamodeling are important in SG development. 2) SG is a significant mechanism for cooperative and participative learning strategies. 3) Virtual Reality (VR) technology is widely used in safe virtual environments for mining training. 4) There is a need for methodologies that integrate the specification of cognitive functions with the affective part of the users for SGs suitable for learning environments.
